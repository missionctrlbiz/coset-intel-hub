Executive Summary

For decades, gas flaring has remained a persistent challenge in Nigeria’s Niger Delta, representing not only a significant waste of natural resources but also a severe environmental and public health crisis. This intelligence report examines the socio-ecological ramifications of continuous flaring, analyzing its contribution to regional climate change, acid rain, and the systemic respiratory issues among local populations. We propose a multi-tiered regulatory framework to transition towards gas reinjection and commercialization.
